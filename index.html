About the Summit

A Working Summit on the Grand Challenges in
Information Science and Scholarly Communications

The Summitâ€™s purpose will be to identify and prioritize specific grand challenges in each of the following targeted areas of the information sciences and scholarly communications:

    1) scholarly discovery

    2) digital curation and preservation

    3) open scholarship

The identification of the central questions within each Challenge area will pave the way for building an information science and scholarly communications research agenda for the information science and library research community over the next five to ten years; and the Summit will establish connections among stakeholders and innovators from across disciplines that promote future

collaboration.

Keynote Speakers

Dr. Carla Hayden

Carla Hayden was sworn in as the 14th Librarian of Congress on September 14, 2016. Hayden, the first woman and the first African American to lead the national library, was nominated to the position by President Barack Obama on February 24, 2016, and her nomination was confirmed by the U.S. Senate on July 13. 

Joi Ito

Joi Ito is the director of the MIT Media Lab, Professor of the Practice at MIT and the author, with Jeff Howe, of Whiplash: How to Survive Our Faster Future (Grand Central Publishing, 2016).


Anasuya Sengupta

Anasuya Sengupta is Co-Director of
Whose Knowledge? whoseknowledge.org 
She has led initiatives in India and the USA, across the global South, and internationally for over 20 years, to amplify marginalised voices in virtual and physical worlds.
